Driving Regulations

General Conduct

  • Fair Play: Intentional crashing, blocking, or race manipulation is grounds for disciplinary action.
  • Clean Racing: Reckless driving will be penalised.

Race Etiquette

  • Track Limits: In-game track limits apply. No stewards overrides.
  • Rejoining Safely: Must rejoin safely after going off-track.
  • Reset to track allowed only if stuck. Causing damage will be penalized.
  • Blue Flags: Yield to faster cars. No impeding.
  • Yellow Flags: Slow down and do not overtake unless safe.

Qualifying Rules

  • Impedance: Blocking is prohibited.
  • Out/In Laps: Must yield to flying laps.
  • Retiring: Retire off racing line safely.

Overtaking and Defending

  • Racing Room: Leave space for others.
  • Defensive Moves: Only one move allowed per straight.
  • Dive Bombs: Must be clean or will be penalised.

Starts and Safety Cars

  • Race Starts: No false starts.
  • Safety Car: No overtaking unless instructed.
  • Maintain gaps, avoid erratic driving.
  • No strategic manipulation of safety cars.

Penalties and Stewarding

  • Incident Reports: Must be submitted within 24 hours with video evidence.
  • Stewards Panel: 3 impartial stewards review incidents.
  • Penalty Types:
  • Time penalties
  • Qualifying bans
  • Race bans or suspensions

Connection and Technical Issues

  • Lagging/Glitching Cars: Fix issues before returning.

Car Setup and Assists

  • Custom Setups: Allowed.
  • Parc Fermé: Must follow in-game regulations.

Mass Disconnections

Within Opening 25% of racing laps
- If 20%+ drivers disconnect, restart the race. Use original grid and weather.

After 25% of racing laps
- Use red flag rules and restart if needed. Half or full points may be awarded based on distance covered. PS5/4 prioritized if crossplay is the issue.

Point Scoring

Real-life F1 point system: 25-18-15-12-10-8-6-4-2-1
No points for fastest lap (F1 2025).
Tiebreaks go to most wins → poles → fastest laps.
